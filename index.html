How I Think

My Creative Framework

Every winning creative follows the same disciplined path, from insight to scale. This is the system behind the 30-35% winner rate.

01
Consumer Research

Understand the audience's real pains, desires, and the language they use, before any concept exists.

02
Angle Development

Translate consumer insight into distinct strategic angles, each with a clear hypothesis to test.

03
Hook Testing

Validate the opening seconds first. A weak hook means the rest of the creative never gets seen.

04
Creative Iteration

Read the Meta KPIs, isolate what worked, and iterate on the winners with sharper variants.

05
Scaling Winners

Push proven creatives to scale while protecting efficiency, then feed learnings back into the next round.

The framework, applied

For the prostate health brand: research showed men over 40 blamed their symptoms on aging, not a condition → so I built an education-first angle to create problem awareness → tested hooks that led with the symptom, not the product → found that messaging around control outperformed messaging around relief, because the real desire was dignity → scaled that angle to six figures in monthly spend.

The audience didn't know they had a treatable condition. So the creative had to teach before it could sell.

A prostate health supplement brand needed a scalable creative system on Meta. The challenge: the target audience, men aged 40 to 65, was largely unaware they had a diagnosable, treatable condition. They thought it was just aging.

I built a full creative testing architecture across six distinct psychological angles, each carrying a clear hypothesis before a single frame was designed. The age-targeted education angle was designed to create problem awareness first; the "regain control" messaging was built on a deeper insight, that the real desire isn't symptom relief but dignity and agency.

  • Age-targeted problem education and awareness

  • Identity-level desire: control and dignity

In wellness, credibility converts. So the founder's face became the creative.

A wellness and lifestyle brand needed leads, not direct purchases, a different objective that calls for a different creative approach. I built a founder-led video strategy, using the founder's face to build authenticity and trust for a category where credibility drives conversion.

I tested multiple founder-led video angles and optimised heavily on hook rate, the percentage of viewers retained in the opening seconds, since a strong hook is what makes or breaks a lead-gen video. The best variant reached a 54% hook rate.

A high cost per click isn't always a problem. Sometimes it's the creative filtering out the wrong buyer.

A premium lingerie brand needed creatives that matched its positioning while still driving conversions. The challenge with premium pricing is that not every click should convert.

I developed a multi-angle static strategy covering before/after transformation, social proof at scale, direct comparison against regular bras, and aspirational product imagery. One premium product creative carried a higher cost per result (Rs 800+) yet still returned 4x ROAS, because its premium visual language naturally filtered for high-intent, quality-led buyers rather than bargain hunters.

  • Before/after visual transformation proof
  • Comparison vs regular bra, category elevation
  • Premium product visual: Rs 800+ CPR at 4x ROAS
